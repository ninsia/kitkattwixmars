Planets:

A planet is a celestial body that orbits a star. Planets have enough mass to approach a round shape, but too little mass to cause nuclear fusion within them, so that they do not emit light themselves. In the solar system, eight planets orbit the Sun. The Earth is most likely the only one on which life exists.

Origin of Planets:

Planets form from protoplanetary disks. The largest part of such a disk clumps together in the center to form a star. The remaining gas and dust slowly forms into asteroids, which later develop into planets. In young planets close to the star, the light gases can evaporate, leaving a rocky core.For planets further from the star, this process does not take place or is much slower.
